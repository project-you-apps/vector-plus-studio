# Tycho Brahe

Danish astronomer. Built the most precise pre-telescope observational
instruments of the 16th century at his observatory Uraniborg, on the
island of Hven. His decades of naked-eye measurements of stellar and
planetary positions formed the empirical bedrock that [[Johannes Kepler]]
later used to derive the [[Heliocentrism|laws of planetary motion]].

## Observational precision

Brahe's instruments achieved positional accuracy of approximately one
arcminute — roughly the resolution limit of the human eye. This was an
order of magnitude better than any prior systematic survey. His catalog
of 777 stars (later expanded to 1,000+) was the most accurate in the
world until well after the [[Telescope]] entered general use.

The Mars observations specifically — small but persistent discrepancies
between observation and the assumed circular orbit — were what allowed
Kepler to deduce that orbits are *elliptical* rather than circular.
Without Tycho's precision, the ellipse could not have been distinguished
from a slightly off-center circle. #observation #precision

## The Tychonic system

Tycho proposed his own model of the cosmos, a hybrid: the Earth at the
center (as in [[Geocentrism]]), with the Sun and Moon orbiting the Earth,
but all other planets orbiting the Sun. This was geometrically equivalent
to [[Heliocentrism|Copernican]] heliocentrism but preserved the
theological priority of a stationary Earth. #model #compromise

## Connections

- [[Johannes Kepler]] joined Tycho as assistant in 1600; after Tycho's
  death in 1601 Kepler used Tycho's Mars data to derive the laws of
  planetary motion
- [[Galileo Galilei]] never met Tycho but used the Tychonic data
  indirectly through Kepler's published work

## Famously

Tycho lost part of his nose in a duel and wore a metal prosthetic for
the rest of his life. He kept a tame elk at Uraniborg that died falling
down a staircase after drinking too much beer. #trivia
