# Galileo Galilei

Italian astronomer, physicist, and engineer. Sometimes called the father of
observational astronomy and modern physics. Champion of [[Heliocentrism]]
during a period when [[Geocentrism]] was the official position of the
Catholic Church.

## Telescope work

In 1609 Galileo improved on existing Dutch designs of the [[Telescope]],
building instruments capable of ~30x magnification. Within months he had
made discoveries that fundamentally undermined the [[Geocentrism|earth-centered]]
model:

- **Jovian moons** — four bodies orbiting Jupiter, proof that not everything
  orbits the Earth
- **Phases of Venus** — full set of phases visible only if Venus orbits the Sun
- **Lunar mountains** — the Moon was not a perfect celestial sphere
- **Sunspots** — the Sun itself was not perfect and unchanging

These observations are recorded in *Sidereus Nuncius* (1610). #publication

## Conflict with the Church

Galileo's advocacy for the [[Heliocentrism|Copernican system]] led to his
trial by the Roman Inquisition in 1633. He was found "vehemently suspect of
heresy" and spent the rest of his life under house arrest. The Church
formally retracted its position in 1992. #conflict #church

## Connections

- [[Johannes Kepler]] corresponded with Galileo and independently advanced
  the heliocentric case via his three laws of planetary motion
- [[Tycho Brahe]]'s observational data underpinned much of Kepler's work,
  which in turn supported Galileo's telescopic findings

## Quote

> "And yet it moves." — *E pur si muove*, attributed (apocryphally) to
> Galileo after his recantation. ^famous-quote
