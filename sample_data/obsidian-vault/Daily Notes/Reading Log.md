# Reading Log

Running notes on what I'm reading in the Renaissance-astronomy domain.
Linked from the [[Index]] and cross-referenced into the people and
concept notes as relevant.

## 2026-04-03

Started Koestler's *The Sleepwalkers* — a triple biography of [[Tycho Brahe]],
[[Johannes Kepler]], and [[Galileo Galilei]]. Koestler is unkind to Galileo
(portrays him as politically tone-deaf) and surprisingly sympathetic to
Kepler's mysticism. The framing of [[Heliocentrism]] as a "sleepwalking"
discovery — accidents of personality and politics rather than clean
deductive triumph — is interesting but possibly oversold. #book

## 2026-04-12

Reread the section on [[Tycho Brahe]]'s observatory at Uraniborg. The
precision of his instruments — sub-arcminute on positional measurements
— is genuinely staggering for a pre-[[Telescope]] era. The fact that
[[Johannes Kepler|Kepler]] inherited that data and used it to derive
elliptical orbits is the cleanest example of "data quality unlocks
theoretical advance" I can think of. #methodology #observation

## 2026-04-19

Watched a lecture on Kepler's *Mysterium Cosmographicum* (his 1596 book
proposing planetary spacing via nested Platonic solids). Wrong, but the
methodology — testing a mathematical hypothesis against observational
data — is recognizably modern. The fact that he abandoned the model when
it didn't fit makes him more scientific than many of his contemporaries.
#mysticism #methodology
