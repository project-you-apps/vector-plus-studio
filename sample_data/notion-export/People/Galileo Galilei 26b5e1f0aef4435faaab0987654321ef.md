# Galileo Galilei

Born: February 15, 1564
Died: January 8, 1642
Tags: people, italian, astronomer, physicist
Aliases: Galileo

Italian astronomer, physicist, and engineer. Sometimes called the father
of observational astronomy and modern physics. Champion of heliocentrism
during a period when geocentrism was the official position of the
Catholic Church.

## Telescope work

In 1609 Galileo improved on existing Dutch designs of the telescope,
building instruments capable of ~30x magnification. Within months he had
made discoveries that fundamentally undermined the earth-centered model:

- [x] Jovian moons — four bodies orbiting Jupiter
- [x] Phases of Venus — Venus orbits the Sun
- [x] Lunar mountains — the Moon was not a perfect sphere
- [x] Sunspots — the Sun is not unchanging

These observations were recorded in *Sidereus Nuncius* (1610).

## Conflict with the Church

> 💡 The Inquisition trial of 1633 found Galileo "vehemently suspect of
> heresy." He spent the rest of his life under house arrest. The Church
> formally retracted its position in 1992.

## Famous quote

> "And yet it moves." — *E pur si muove*, attributed (apocryphally) to
> Galileo after his recantation.
