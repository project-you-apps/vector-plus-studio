# Heliocentrism

Tags: concept, astronomy, paradigm
Aliases: Copernican system, sun-centered model

The astronomical model in which the Sun sits at the center of the solar
system and the planets — including Earth — orbit around it. Replaced
geocentrism as the consensus model over the course of the 16th and
17th centuries.

## Historical sketch

- 3rd century BCE: Aristarchus of Samos proposes a heliocentric model; rejected
- 1543: Nicolaus Copernicus publishes *De revolutionibus orbium coelestium*
- 1609: Galileo Galilei uses the telescope, observes Jovian moons + Venus phases
- 1609-1619: Johannes Kepler derives the three laws of planetary motion
- 1687: Newton's *Principia* explains Kepler's laws via universal gravitation

## Why it took so long

Three challenges had to be overcome:

1. **Stellar parallax** — small effect, not measured until 1838
2. **Falling-object problem** — pre-Newtonian physics couldn't explain it
3. **Religious authority** — Catholic Church held geocentrism as required
